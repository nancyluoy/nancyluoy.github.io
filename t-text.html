Project Overview

T-Text is an entrepreneurial platform that aims to help students to stay connected with their university. The founder of T-Text wants a mobile application that allows students to be connected with their university and have access to resources and social events efficiently and adequately.

Challenge

The Problems

Many students have difficulty accessing resources or are unaware of the events occurring because of their busy lives.

  • Is there a study group for this course I am taking?​​
  • what are some clubs at my school?
  • where do I find information about upcoming events?
  • what are the hours of the library?​​
  • what campus resources are available?
  • where and who do I go for a specific question?
  • where do I find or sell second-hand textbooks and furniture?
The Opportunity

Currently university students use several different systems for content-sharing with other students, online marketplace shopping, and event planning. T-Text seeks to aggregate these use cases into a single platform. ​

​How might we create a simple and easy to use mobile app that satisfies all the needs?

Process

Defining Requirements

Working in intense collaboration with the founder, we had planning meetings twice a week and follow-ups to understand business/user requirements, define priorities, and set product goal.

Basic Requirements
  • The app shall contain A general feed where students could post anything appropriate and ask questions​​
  • The app shall contain a place for merchandise exchanging
  • The app shall contain lists of all campus resources and clubs
  • the app shall contain an in-app search engine​​
  • The app shall support direct messaging between users
  • Users shall be able to post services that they offer or request services they need
  • Users shall be able to view upcoming events.

Prototype & Validate

How do I include all the features wanted while keeping the interface clean?

I created low-fidelity prototypes to iterate and communicate the thought process and strategy to the client. I also created a couple of hi-fidelity prototypes to provide a better visualization of the design.

Final Designs

After validating it with the client, I moved to a well-defined and polished version of the interface.

Outcome

The application is currently still under development. It would’ve been great to do some user testing but there wasn’t the time or budget. If I had more time to work on the project, I would definitely conduct some user testings.

Learnings & Reflection

This project gave me valuable experience of working with a client to deliver a brand-new mobile application in a short time scale and with a limited budget. I learned to collaborate and exchange ideas with people who are not familiar with software development at all. I also realized the importance of timely communication in a software development process. I was able to establish strong communication channels with the client and the developers to make sure we are on the right track and ensure valuable time is not lost.
